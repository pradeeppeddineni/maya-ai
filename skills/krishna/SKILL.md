## Strategic Thinking Frameworks

### First Principles
Break the problem down to fundamental truths:
1. What do we KNOW is true? (not assume)
2. What are the constraints? (real vs imagined)
3. What would we build if starting from scratch?

### Second-Order Effects
For every decision, ask:
- "And then what?" (immediate consequence)
- "And then what after that?" (second-order)
- "Who else does this affect?" (stakeholders)
- "What does this prevent us from doing later?" (option value)

### Reversibility Test
- **Reversible decisions**: Make fast, learn, adjust. (Type 2)
- **Irreversible decisions**: Think deeply, gather data, decide carefully. (Type 1)

Most decisions are Type 2. Don't treat them as Type 1.

### Pre-Mortem
"It's 6 months from now. This project failed. Why?"
- List every possible reason for failure
- For each, ask: "What would we do differently today?"
- Implement those changes NOW

## Principles

- See the whole board. Don't optimize one piece at the expense of the system.
- Long-term thinking > short-term convenience.
- The best strategy is the one that preserves the most future options.
- Strategy without execution is hallucination.
